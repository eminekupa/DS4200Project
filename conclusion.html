Closing the Loop: What We Learned About Mediterranean Hotel Cancellations

From Channels to Customers: A Journey Through Cancellations

This project began with a simple question: why do hotel bookings get canceled? Over the course of the semester, we examined this question through the lens of over 22,000 bookings across Spain, France, and Italy â€” three tourism-driven Mediterranean economies.

Our visualization story follows a deliberate narrative arc that progressively deepens the analysis: starting with how people book (channels), moving to who they are (repeat vs. new guests), where they're booking (geographic context), what drives their decisions (pricing and demographics), and ultimately which market segments they belong to. This progression allows us to uncover the multifaceted nature of cancellation behaviors across the Mediterranean region.

What We Found

Booking Channel Sets the Tone

Our first visualization revealed a striking correlation between booking method and cancellation likelihood, with TA/TO channels showing cancellation rates over three times higher than direct bookings.

  • Direct/Corporate bookings: lowest cancellation rates (~8%)
  • TA/TO channels: highest cancellations (~26%)
  • ODS: moderate risk (~12%)
Loyalty Is Stability

Our interactive line plot demonstrated that new guests are significantly more likely to cancel their reservations, while repeat guests demonstrate much stronger booking commitment across all three countries.

  • New guests: 30-40% cancellation rates
  • Repeat guests: under 10% cancellation rates
  • Gap widens during peak seasons
Regional Booking Patterns

Our geographic analysis uncovered distinct cancellation behaviors across countries, hotel types, and booking purposes, with considerable variation based on location and travel intent.

  • City hotels: higher cancellation rates than resorts
  • Business travel: more cancellations than leisure
  • Italy: highest overall cancellation rates
Price Sensitivity and Traveler Types

Our interactive visualization of room rates and traveler demographics revealed clear patterns in cancellation behavior, with certain traveler types showing significantly higher price sensitivity.

  • Couples: highest cancellation rates (~70%)
  • Seasonal pricing affects cancellation patterns
  • Solo travelers: most stable bookings
Market Segment Vulnerabilities

Our market segmentation analysis identified specific high-risk booking combinations that hotels should monitor closely to improve booking reliability and revenue forecasting.

  • Aviation/Group: highest risk in Spain
  • Online TA/Transient: problematic across all countries
  • Contract bookings: generally more reliable
  • Country-specific patterns require targeted strategies

Connecting the Narrative

By understanding these cancellation patterns, our visualization story provides insights that hotel managers can use to better predict booking reliability, improve revenue forecasting, and develop targeted retention strategies for the Mediterranean market. Our progression from booking channels to customer segments creates a comprehensive view of what drives cancellation behaviors.

Final Reflection

Our analysis reveals that cancellation behaviors in Mediterranean hotels are influenced by multiple interconnected factors. Booking channels significantly impact reliability, with direct bookings proving most stable. Customer loyalty emerges as perhaps the strongest predictor of booking commitment, with repeat guests consistently showing lower cancellation rates. Geographic and seasonal patterns vary considerably, with city hotels and business travelers demonstrating higher cancellation tendencies than resort and leisure bookings. Demographic analysis shows couples are surprisingly more likely to cancel than other traveler types. Finally, our market segmentation uncovers specific high-risk combinations like Online TA/Transient customers that require targeted retention strategies. These findings provide hotel managers with actionable insights to develop differentiated pricing, overbooking policies, and marketing strategies tailored to each segment's distinct cancellation risk profile.
