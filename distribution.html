Distribution Channel Impact

Setting the Stage: Where and How Guests Book Their Hotel Stay

Why Start with Distribution Channels?

In our investigation of hotel booking cancellations, we begin with a simple but crucial question: Does the way a guest books their stay affect the likelihood of cancellation?

Understanding this relationship is essential for hotel revenue managers and operations teams. Booking platforms—whether direct, third-party, or travel agent—introduce varying levels of uncertainty, customer loyalty, and cancellation risk.

In this section, we:

  • Explore cancellation trends across booking sources using a clear D3 bar chart
  • Compare cancellation rates between channels to identify high-risk segments
  • Use this insight to frame our next visualizations on guest behavior and demographics

Figure 1: Cancellation Rate by Distribution Channel (Spain)

Key Insight

Booking Source Matters

Our chart clearly shows that Travel Agent bookings are the most likely to be canceled, followed closely by Online Travel Agencies (OTAs). In contrast, Direct bookings have the lowest cancellation rate, signaling a higher level of guest commitment.

  • Travel Agent: Highest cancellation rate — over 50%
  • Direct Bookings: Lowest cancellation rate — under 25%
  • OTA: Moderate but significant cancellation risk

These trends reinforce the idea that hotels should promote direct booking through loyalty programs or exclusive perks to reduce cancellation risk and increase revenue reliability.
